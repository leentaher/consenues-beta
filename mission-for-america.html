MFA at a Glance

A narrative vision of what it would look like if an independent president responded to the AI crisis and climate catastrophe by launching a WWII-scale mission to rebuild the American economy.

The RFC

Reviving the Reconstruction Finance Corporation as a public investment bank to finance and coordinate the massive industrial mobilization the Mission for America requires.

read chapter → chapter 02

Clean Power

Transitioning to 100% clean electricity and doubling total power production in 10 years to support the electrification of transportation, industry, and buildings across America.

read chapter → chapter 03

EVs

Producing 30 million electric vehicles a year so America becomes the world's top exporter — with the manufacturing base, supply chains, and charging network to match.

read chapter → chapter 04

Steel

Expanding domestic steel output by 50% and converting the entire industry to clean production. The backbone of the new economy — made here, made clean.

read chapter → chapter 05

Hydrogen

A national green hydrogen program to decarbonize everything electrification can't reach — heavy industry, long-haul transport, chemicals, and beyond.

read chapter → chapter 06

Buildings

Retrofitting every existing home and building millions of new zero-emission homes to solve the housing crisis and the climate crisis simultaneously.

read chapter → chapter 07

Aircraft

A moonshot for zero-emissions aviation — hydrogen-powered long-haul jets, electric short-haul, and a modernized aerospace industry built around it.

read chapter → chapter 08

Trucking

Transitioning the American trucking fleet to all-electric, with a nationwide fast-charging network designed for freight from day one.

read chapter → chapter 09

Shipping

Clean ports. Hydrogen-powered ships. A reindustrialized U.S. shipbuilding sector built to serve the global transition — and export it.

read chapter → chapter 10

Farming

Electrify farms. Cut methane. Reform subsidies so American agriculture rewards stewardship, resilience, and clean production at scale.

read chapter → chapter 11

Geothermal

Unlocking virtually inexhaustible, always-on clean energy — everywhere in the country — using next-generation enhanced geothermal systems.

read chapter → chapter 12

Manhattan Projects

Full-stack national programs to crack the hardest remaining problems: atmospheric CO₂ removal, methane, and zero-emission concrete.

read chapter → chapter 13

Nuclear

Deploying advanced reactors on military bases to bypass permitting gridlock and demonstrate a new generation of firm, clean baseload power.

read chapter → chapter 14

Water

New aqueducts, reservoirs, and storage to end the Western water crisis and secure a stable supply for cities, farms, and ecosystems.

read chapter → chapter 15

Toxins

Track every harmful chemical in commerce. Ban the processes responsible for the worst of them. Clean up what's already been done.

read chapter → chapter 16

Public Transport

High-speed rail between every major city. Electric transit inside them. Car-free living made actually practical for tens of millions of Americans.

read chapter → chapter 17

Workforce

30 million new workers trained into the new economy. Paid training, guaranteed healthcare, and childcare so anyone can make the jump.

read chapter → chapter 18

Industry

Modernize and decarbonize every factory — cement, chemicals, paper, glass, aluminum — so American manufacturing leads the clean economy globally.

read chapter → chapter 19

Cooldown

A NASA-led research and deployment program to temporarily cool the planet while emissions come down — carefully, transparently, internationally.

read chapter → chapter 20

Foreign Policy

Finance clean development globally. Align trade, aid, and diplomacy with climate goals so the transition is American-led and worldwide.

read chapter → chapter 21

Oceans

Make U.S. waters the healthiest marine area on Earth — protected fisheries, restored ecosystems, and a living coastline that works for everyone.

Fossil Fuels

A 10-year orderly wind-down of the fossil fuel economy. Workers, communities, and regions supported every step of the way. No chaos.
