they did this on purpose, btw

We used to build things. Industries. A middle class. Then the people in charge got a fun idea: what if we just... stopped trying? Let the market decide. Ship the jobs. Gut the agencies. Tell everyone to "adapt."

Forty years later, here's adaptation: stagnant wages, impossible housing, no savings, and three simultaneous crises arriving at the door they left wide open. Cool cool cool.

anyway we've done this before

In 1942, America transformed its entire economy in under three years. Factories retooled overnight. Twelve million people mobilized. We did it because we had no choice.

We're at that moment again. Except now the enemy is three things at once and also kind of abstract. The Mission for America is a 23-chapter playbook for total mobilization. It's very long. It's very serious. Someone had to write it.

we're the think tank for the emergency

Most think tanks write policy designed to be "realistic." Small ideas that might survive a hostile Congress. That's not us.

We didn't want to be the people writing this either. And yet.
